01 — Vision

The one line
that matters

Boutique operators need a boutique digital ecosystem. Until Terrain, that did not exist.

Terrain builds fully custom, fully integrated digital ecosystems for boutique resort operators across the Western Hemisphere — owned completely by the operator, built specifically for them, and supported by a dedicated long-term concierge.

What we are building
One unified digital ecosystem per client: booking engine, communications, operations, analytics, and AI — all custom-built, all operator-owned, all maintained by a dedicated concierge. No vendor lock-in. No surprise price hikes. No generic tools.
What winning looks like
In year seven, 400 operators across the Western Hemisphere are running their businesses on Terrain. The Cliffs at Hocking Hills is the living proof-of-concept that started it all. Terrain exits at $100M at 8× EBITDA.
This is not SaaS
SaaS is built for the 80%. You are the 20%. Terrain is built exclusively for operators who refuse generic solutions, own their brand, and invest in differentiation.

07 — Compensation

How Tim gets paid

Tim's equity and his compensation are entirely separate conversations. Equity is his co-founder stake — long-term wealth. Compensation is fair market pay for the work he does every day.

Phase 1 — Part-time (now through full-time trigger) Tim receives 50% of total monthly revenue — recurring fees plus build fee payments — as direct compensation. This is in addition to any distributions; salary and distributions are separate and cumulative. The remaining 50% is retained by the company as operating reserves and capital, and may only be distributed upon mutual written agreement of both founding parties.
50% of revenue
Full-time trigger When Tim's 50% revenue share annualizes to $250,000 per year — approximately $20,833/month to Tim, requiring ~$41,667/month in total Terrain revenue. At ~$5,000/client average (recurring + build financing), this triggers at roughly 8–9 clients. Happens organically, not arbitrarily.
~8–9 clients
Full-time salary — $250K minimum, not a ceiling Once the trigger is reached, salary is set at $250,000 per year as a guaranteed floor regardless of how early it happens and regardless of temporary revenue fluctuation. This salary is in addition to any profit distributions — not a substitute for them. As the business scales, salary is subject to upward adjustment by mutual agreement of both parties.
$250K min/year
Profit distributions Distributions are separate from and in addition to Tim's salary. Net profits available for distribution are allocated proportionally per ownership stake: Tim 40%, Kimball + Christine 40%, The Cliffs LLC 20%. Distributions require mutual agreement of both voting parties and are paid on a cadence to be agreed (quarterly recommended).
40 / 40 / 20
Why this structure works: Tim sees real money from day one — not a promise of future payment. The 50% revenue model protects him in early months when revenue is small and rewards him immediately as it grows. His salary is a guaranteed floor, not a cap — it rises with the business. Salary and distributions are additive, not interchangeable. The remaining 50% of early revenue stays in the company as protected reserves, distributed only when both parties agree. Equity upside compounds independently of all compensation. Tim is not taking a pay cut in exchange for equity — he is being paid fairly for his work and building long-term wealth.

10 — Buyer Value-Adds

Two levers we leave for the buyer

Terrain exits as a focused, proven business. We deliberately leave two major expansion opportunities untouched — giving any acquirer immediate, de-risked growth playbooks.

01
Property Management Companies
What it isProfessional PM companies managing 25–150+ units. Different buyer profile, different sales motion, same core platform. Why we leave itServing PM companies requires different concierge workflows, different build scope, and different pricing. Adding it during the Terrain growth phase risks diluting the boutique resort brand. A buyer with capital can activate this segment immediately post-acquisition. Estimated TAM3,000–5,000 PM companies in the Western Hemisphere alone. Potential to double the client base with no product development required.
02
Full International Expansion
What it isEurope, APAC, Middle East, Africa — boutique resort operators globally who face the same fragmented tool problem Terrain solves. Why we leave itInternational expansion beyond the Western Hemisphere introduces regulatory complexity, language requirements, and significantly higher operational overhead. Terrain's 7-year plan is focused on the English-speaking Western Hemisphere. Full international is a post-exit opportunity. Estimated TAM expansionGlobal boutique resort market is 3–5× the Western Hemisphere market. A strategic acquirer with global infrastructure could deploy Terrain internationally within 12–18 months.
